QuantumData Inc. Reports Breach: 4.7 Million User Records Exposed

news2026-04-09

QuantumData Inc., a cloud analytics firm based in Singapore, disclosed a data breach affecting 4.7 million user accounts. The company says attackers gained unauthorized access to its production database through a compromised third-party integration credential. Exposed data includes names, email addresses, hashed passwords, and partial payment card information.

Timeline of the Incident

According to QuantumData's incident report, the breach began on March 14 when an attacker exploited an improperly scoped API token belonging to a data pipeline vendor. The intrusion went undetected for 11 days before anomaly detection systems flagged abnormal export activity on March 25. The breach was fully contained by March 27.

Regulatory Implications

QuantumData operates across the EU and has notified relevant data protection authorities under GDPR Article 33, which mandates notification within 72 hours of discovery. Fines under GDPR can reach 4% of global annual turnover, placing the company at potential exposure in the tens of millions of euros. Class-action proceedings have been filed in three EU jurisdictions.

Company Response

QuantumData has engaged a third-party forensics firm to conduct a full post-incident review. The company has pledged to complete a comprehensive audit of all third-party integrations and implement zero-trust network segmentation within 90 days of the incident.
